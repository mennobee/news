8 Reasons we don't want a website:

1. The conditioning effect of the screen.
    Investigators have shown it's about as life changing as the invention of cars.
    So, we have placed this handy little print button here to covert those pixels to substance.

(You may now continue reading on your sheet of paper)

2. Prolonged screen-time may be hard on your eyes.
    I'm just soooo relieved to know you're now reading safely on paper!  Hurray!

3. We still want our readers to get something done.
    With new & interesting content being published all the time we have decided it could cause a tendecy
     toward "endless doomscrolling".

4. Websites cost money.
    Who wants to be paying anywhere from $0.00 to $100 a month just to broadcast free content??

5. Websites take work.
    MennoBee authors are also laborers and providers and fishermen and hunters and..................
    Just finding time to write articles is bad enough withough designing websites!!

6. We don't want to get taken offline for spreading MISINFORMATION.

7. We don't want to go viral!
    The Mennonite Grapevine is long and fast and we just really wouldn't want all that attention....

8. Benefits would be limited
    As most of our reader's churches do not allow connection to "information pools" a website just might not get many viewers.
